#melatonin

Cyclical changes that happen on a roughly 24-hour basis in several biological processes in the body, including hormone release, brain waves, body temperature, and drowsiness. This is coordinated by about 20,000 neurons in the hypothalamus, a structure commonly known as the **biological clock**.

The biological clock ticks even inside red blood cells (Edgar et al., 2012).

## Melatonin 

**Melatonin** is the hormone that increases after dark when approaching sleep time, triggering feelings of sleepiness. Plays a key role in regulating circadian rhythms.
