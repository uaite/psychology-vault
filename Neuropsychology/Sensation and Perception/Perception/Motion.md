The brain judges how things are changing by comparing visual frames.

The **phi phenomenon** is the illusory perception of movement produced by successively flashing images. The lights are jumping from one spot to another, but they appear continuous. This illustrates how the brain works only with best guesses.

## Motion blindness

A serious disorder in which patients can't string still images into perceived motion.

This is very debilitating and makes it impossible to do simple things like crossing the street, since the person can't tell if the car far away is incoming.
